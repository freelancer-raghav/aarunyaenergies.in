Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions — Solid Biofuel

Answers to the most common questions about biofuel trading, briquettes, pellets, and biomass fuel supply in India.

What is the calorific value of mustard briquettes?

+

Mustard briquettes have a gross calorific value (GCV) of 3,800 to 4,200 kcal/kg, with moisture content below 12% and ash content of 8–12%. They are a cost-effective substitute for coal in brick kilns and industrial boilers, particularly in Rajasthan and Haryana where mustard cultivation generates abundant crop residue.

What is the difference between biomass briquettes and biomass pellets?

+

Biomass briquettes are larger compressed blocks (60–90 mm diameter), suited for manual-feed boilers, kilns, and furnaces. Biomass pellets are smaller cylindrical pieces (6–8 mm diameter) designed for automated pellet burners and co-firing systems. Pellets generally have lower moisture content and slightly higher calorific value than briquettes of the same feedstock material.

Are biomass briquettes a good alternative to coal?

+

Yes. Biomass briquettes are a renewable, lower-carbon alternative to coal offering calorific values of 3,500–4,500 kcal/kg — comparable to lignite and sub-bituminous coal. They produce significantly less sulphur dioxide, qualify for green energy incentives, and reduce dependence on imported fossil fuels. Many brick kilns, textile units, and food processing plants across India have switched to biomass briquettes as their primary industrial fuel.

What is the minimum order quantity for solid biofuel?

+

The minimum order quantity is 10 metric tons (MT) per consignment. We supply in loose form, bags, or as per client requirement. For regular bulk orders, we offer flexible weekly or monthly supply scheduling to match your boiler's consumption pattern.

Which states in India do you supply biofuels to?

+

We supply solid biofuels — briquettes, pellets, and wood chips — across Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab, and Uttar Pradesh. Delivery is arranged via road transport with complete documentation including invoice, e-way bill, and weight slip.

What are paddy straw pellets used for?

+

Paddy straw pellets are used as biomass fuel in industrial boilers and furnaces for steam and process heat generation. With a calorific value of 3,200–3,600 kcal/kg, they are an economical fuel option. They also help address the paddy stubble burning problem in Punjab and Haryana by converting rice straw waste into usable energy.

What industries use solid biofuels in India?

+

Solid biofuels including briquettes, pellets, and wood chips are widely used by brick kilns, textile and dyeing units, food processing plants, paper and pulp mills, chemical industries, pharmaceutical companies, hotels, and large institutions across India for steam generation, process heating, and kiln firing as a cost-effective alternative to coal and furnace oil.
